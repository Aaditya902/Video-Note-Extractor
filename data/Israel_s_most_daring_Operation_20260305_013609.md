## Summary

Operation Entebbe was a daring Israeli commando raid to rescue hostages from a hijacked Air France flight in Uganda. After the plane landed at Entebbe, hijackers, supported by Ugandan forces, made demands for prisoner releases and ransom. Israel launched a covert operation involving a surprise landing, a fake convoy, and a direct assault on the terminal, successfully neutralizing all hijackers and evacuating most hostages, though at the cost of some lives, including the mission commander.

The approved plan involved a large Israeli commando team covertly infiltrating Entebbe Airport without informing Uganda or other nations. The mission was to take over the entire airport, rescue the hostages, and ensure a rapid, secure extraction.

With 102 hostages secured, commandos evacuated them, destroying Ugandan air force planes on the ground before departure. The 53-minute operation, including 30 minutes of combat, resulted in the deaths of all seven hijackers and 45 Ugandan soldiers. The planes refueled in Kenya and returned to Israel.
